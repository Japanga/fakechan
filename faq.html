Frequently Asked Questions

Welcome to FakeChan's frequently asked questions page!

Basics

What is FakeChan?

FakeChan is a silly attempt at replicating and copying several functions of the popular imageboard 4chan while generally serving as a public file and cloud storage client in it's own right. Users do not need to register to upload/display files and can start posting immediately.
How do I access the boards?

All of the boards are listed at the top of each page. The trailing slash function that renders them as "fakechan/boards/a/", "fakechan/boards/x/" etc. is known to be glitchy and gives 404 errors, be sure to always go back to the right board URL when you are finished using it.
How do I post on FakeChan?

Anyone can post whenever they want to one of the Cloudinary image feeds that are bound straight to the database for the site and display their contents instantly on the boards. Boards with dedicated posting units like /a/ and /b/ have to be manually updated to display the post contents, and you are given warnings about this.
How do I post Anonymously?

Namefagging/identities are not encouraged or allowed on FakeChan, all uploads are automatically classified as being from an "Anonymous" in the code with no current options for attributing them to author aliases aside from comments sections.
Do I have to post an image?

Yes, but you do not need to post text. Posting with text is a special functionality only configured for certain boards.

Does FakeChan have greentexting?

If you use boards with posting configured for them, when it is rendered to the FakeChan Core iFrame display; a code is applied to automatically make it

>greentext
Are tripcodes going to be added?

They will be studied in the future like most 4chan functionalities.

What are "GETs"?

GETS/digits are a well known phenomenon on 4chan where the numbers on the end of your post ID have a matching number like 00/88/666/7777/99999 etc. GETS often add more validity and truth to the post, with jokes like "Dubs confirm" as if the post having a GET signifies the quality and status of the post. Theories that webadmins can rig them have existed for years but the truth is you can get them here as well as lots of other similar post ID-based sites like Rule 34. They are handled by the web admin approving/declining posts and could very likely be intentional or by pure luck and chance. Scripts exist to get all of the latets posts across all the 4chan boards to predict when the next GET is going to be. FakeChan has a special board designed solely for tracking GETs on image uploads called /get/

Contribution

What is considered positive contribution?

FakeChan is a meant to be a happy and friendly experience for archiving meetups and files/videos sent in from friends that also came from 4chan as well as giving the public more education on the process of making and running imageboards, and we are not interested in hosting or achiving files born from hostility or meant to antagonize or attack others. We all just want to have a good time here, damn it!
Why is contribution so important?

We want good and consistent users that publish good stuff we would want to show off and host. If it's gross, bad, or unfunny: we don't want it on here! Curating the overall theme of the material hosted here is very important. Classification/organization is also important to make sure all of the materials are being sent to the right boards.

Technical

How does FakeChan run and operate?

FakeChan was initally primarily powered on .HTML/JavaScript and .CSS with an external third party service called Cloudinary to host and retrieve user content and display it to the site. After the creation of FakeChan: Server, a deeper inner node.js framework referred to as "FakeChan: Core" was built to procedurally render upload data to Cloudflare sites and stream the data to FakeChan. Most of FakeChan is open-source, but the scripts for generating/compiling FakeChan boards are privately owned and not available to the public.
